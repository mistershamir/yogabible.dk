About Hot Yoga

What is hot yoga?
Hot yoga is yoga practiced in a heated room, typically between 30-40°C (86-104°F). At Hot Yoga Copenhagen, we use infrared heating panels that warm the body directly rather than the air, creating a comfortable 30-35°C environment. The heat helps increase flexibility, promotes deeper stretching, and encourages detoxification through sweating. Our classes are suitable for all levels, from complete beginners to advanced practitioners.
What is the difference between infrared hot yoga and Bikram yoga?
Infrared hot yoga (what we offer at Hot Yoga Copenhagen) uses infrared heating panels that warm your body directly, similar to sunlight but without UV rays. The room temperature is 30-35°C. Bikram yoga uses traditional heating (forced hot air) to heat the room to 40°C and follows a fixed sequence of 26 poses. Our infrared classes offer varied styles (vinyasa, yin, power, sculpt) at a more comfortable temperature, while still providing the benefits of heat: improved flexibility, deeper stretching, and enhanced circulation.
What are the benefits of hot yoga?
Hot yoga offers numerous benefits: (1) Increased flexibility — warm muscles stretch more easily and safely. (2) Enhanced detoxification — sweating helps flush toxins. (3) Improved cardiovascular health — the heat elevates heart rate similar to moderate cardio. (4) Stress reduction — the combination of heat and yoga promotes deep relaxation. (5) Stronger muscles — holding poses in heat requires more effort. (6) Better sleep — regular practice improves sleep quality. (7) Weight management — a 60-minute class can burn 400-600 calories. At Hot Yoga Copenhagen, our infrared heating provides these benefits at a comfortable 30-35°C.
Is hot yoga good for beginners?
Yes, hot yoga is excellent for beginners. At Hot Yoga Copenhagen, we offer dedicated Hot Yoga Basics classes designed specifically for newcomers. The infrared heat (30-35°C) actually helps beginners because warm muscles are more flexible, making poses more accessible. We recommend starting with our KickStarter trial (10 classes in 3 weeks for 599 DKK) or a single drop-in class (299 DKK). Our teachers provide modifications for all levels in every class.

What should I wear to a hot yoga class?
Wear light, breathable, moisture-wicking clothing. Most people wear shorts and a tank top or sports bra. Cotton absorbs sweat and becomes heavy, so synthetic or bamboo fabrics work best. Bring a water bottle (at least 1 liter) and arrive 10-15 minutes early for your first class. We have showers, changing rooms, and a lounge with complimentary herbal tea and fruit after class.
